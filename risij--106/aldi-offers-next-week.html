Module 01: Aldi Specialbuys Protocol

Last Updated: Nov 01, 2024 Protocol ID: ALD-01

This document outlines the execution protocol for acquiring high-value assets from the Aldi "Specialbuys" middle aisle. Success is dependent on pre-planning and efficient execution. Access the primary Aldi offers next week data feed for target identification.

Phase 1: Pre-Mission Intel

Asset acquisition failure is most often due to poor intelligence. The Aldi data leaflet is released approximately one week prior to deployment. Your primary objective is to parse this data and identify 1-2 primary targets. Proceeding without a clear target list is inefficient and results in suboptimal resource allocation.

Phase 2: Temporal Strategy

New asset drops occur on Thursday and Sunday mornings. High-demand assets are depleted rapidly. Optimal execution requires interfacing with the retail environment at T-zero (opening time) on the day of the drop. This is the single most critical variable for mission success.

Execution Steps: In-Store Protocol

  1. Pathing Algorithm: Upon entry, bypass standard grocery sectors and proceed directly to the central aisle coordinates. Standard grocery procurement is a secondary objective.
  2. Target Acquisition: Locate and secure the primary target(s). Limit acquisition to identified targets to avoid scope creep.
  3. Secondary Objective - "Super 6": Post-acquisition, proceed to the fresh produce sector and acquire the six discounted fruit and vegetable assets known as the "Super 6". This is a standing order for all Aldi missions. Cross-reference with the Morrisons offers this week data feed to ensure total market optimization.
