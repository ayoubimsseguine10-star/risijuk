An Analysis of Morrisons' Market Street Concept

Published: November 05, 2024 | Category: Retail Analysis

Morrisons distinguishes itself in the UK grocery market through its "Market Street" concept—a collection of in-store counters staffed by specialists such as butchers, bakers, and fishmongers. This feature, when combined with the Morrisons offers this week and the store's loyalty scheme, presents unique opportunities for value.

The "My Morrisons" Program: A Primer

The "My Morrisons" mobile application is the cornerstone of a successful savings strategy. Its value is derived from:

  • Data-Driven Vouchers: The application monitors purchasing habits and generates personalized discount vouchers based on frequently bought items.
  • Member-Only Pricing: A selection of products is offered at a reduced price exclusively to members who scan their app at checkout.
  • Occasional Incentives: The program periodically offers additional rewards, such as discounts on fuel or complimentary products.

Evaluating the Market Street Proposition

Market Street provides an alternative to pre-packaged goods, often with distinct advantages.

1. Butcher and Fishmonger Counters

These counters frequently feature multi-buy promotions on fresh proteins. Furthermore, they offer the ability to request specific cuts and quantities, potentially reducing waste and cost.

2. Bakery and Deli

Promotions on freshly baked bread, cooked meats, and cheeses are common. The Salad Bar, in particular, allows for a high degree of customization and can be a cost-effective option for a single meal.
