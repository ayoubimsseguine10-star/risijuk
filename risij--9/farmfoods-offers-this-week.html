The Farmfoods Formula: Big Brands, Low Prices

Published: October 28, 2024 | Category: Supermarket Strategy

Farmfoods operates on a simple, powerful model: offer big-brand products, particularly frozen goods, in multi-buy formats that provide exceptional value. While they may not have the vast range of a hypermarket, they are specialists in their niche. Understanding their approach is key to unlocking the potential of the Farmfoods offers this week.

The Voucher and Multi-Buy System

Farmfoods' primary saving mechanic revolves around two things: their famous multi-buy deals (e.g., "3 for £5") and money-off vouchers that are delivered via mail or available to sign up for online.

Your Farmfoods Savings Strategy

  • Sign Up for Vouchers: The first step for any Farmfoods shopper should be to sign up on their website. They will periodically send you vouchers (e.g., "£5 off a £50 spend") that provide a significant discount on your total shop.
  • Focus on Case Deals: For maximum savings, look for deals on entire cases of products. If you have the freezer space, this is where the deepest per-unit discounts are found.
  • Check the Leaflet: Like other supermarkets, their offers are detailed in a leaflet, which we host on Risij.uk. Use it to plan your bulk buys.
  • Don't Overlook Groceries: While known for frozen food, Farmfoods also has excellent deals on branded groceries like cereals, sauces, and drinks.

The Smart Freezer-Filler

A planned trip to Farmfoods every few weeks is an excellent strategy for stocking your freezer and pantry with staples. This reduces the need for expensive top-up shops at convenience stores like SPAR, saving you a significant amount of money over the long term.
