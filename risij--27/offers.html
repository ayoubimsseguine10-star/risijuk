Offer Type Index

This document outlines the primary promotional mechanics used by major UK supermarkets. Each system is designed to drive consumer behavior. Our analysis provides links to detailed briefs and the live data feed on Risij.uk.

Discount Retailer Mechanics

Primary strategy is based on limited-time, high-demand product drops and aggressive pricing on fresh goods.

  • Lidl: Rotational non-food items ("Middle of Lidl") and themed food weeks. See full analysis: Lidl offers this week.
  • Aldi: Bi-weekly "Specialbuys" (Thurs, Sun) create urgency. See forward intelligence: Aldi offers for next week.

Major Multiples: Loyalty-Based Pricing

The "Big Four" utilize loyalty programs to offer tiered pricing, rewarding repeat customers with lower prices.

  • Tesco: "Clubcard Prices" are the de facto standard price for members. Analysis: Tesco offers guide.
  • ASDA: "Rollback" indicates a long-term price reduction. Analysis: ASDA offers this week.
  • Sainsbury's: "Nectar Prices" function similarly to Tesco's system. Analysis: Sainsbury's offers.
  • Morrisons: "My Morrisons" provides personalized digital coupons. Analysis: Morrisons intelligence.

Specialist & Niche Retailers

These retailers focus on specific categories, from bulk goods to convenience items.

  • Costco: Membership-based warehouse model for bulk purchasing. Analysis: Costco UK offers guide.
  • Iceland & Farmfoods: Focus on aggressive multi-buys for frozen goods. See analysis for Iceland and Farmfoods.
  • Co-op & Spar: Convenience model with member-pricing and targeted weekly deals. See analysis for Co-op and Spar.
