Is a Costco UK Membership Worth It?

Simple Guide | Updated: October 2024

You've probably heard of Costco, the huge warehouse store where you buy things in bulk. But to shop there, you need to pay for a yearly membership. So, is it worth it? This guide breaks down the Costco UK offers to help you decide.

Three Ways You Save at Costco

If you decide to get a membership, your savings will come from three main areas:

  • The Coupon Book: Every month, Costco has a book of deals. You don't need to cut them out; the discounts are applied automatically for members. This is where the biggest savings are.
  • The Kirkland Brand: Costco's own brand is called Kirkland Signature. It's known for being very high quality (often as good as big-name brands) but at a much lower price.
  • Buying in Bulk: This is the main idea of Costco. You buy bigger packs of things you use all the time, which means the price for each individual item is lower.

A Simple Plan for Smart Costco Shopping

To make sure your membership pays for itself, follow these three easy tips:

  1. Plan Around the Coupons: Before you go, look at the monthly coupon book online. The Risij.uk Costco page has a good summary. Only buy the discounted things you actually need.
  2. Make Sure You Have Space: Buying in bulk is great, but only if you have room to store it all! Check your cupboards and freezer space before you shop.
  3. Check the "Per Unit" Price: Sometimes a big pack isn't actually cheaper. Do a quick check in your head of the price per loo roll or per kilo to make sure you're getting a good deal compared to a regular supermarket.

Good for Big Stock-Ups

Costco isn't meant to replace your normal trip to Sainsbury's for milk and bread. It's for big, planned shopping trips to stock up on household essentials. If you have the space and stick to a plan, the membership can easily save you money over the year.
