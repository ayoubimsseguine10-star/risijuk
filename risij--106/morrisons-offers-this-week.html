Module 06: Morrisons Market St. Protocol

Last Updated: Nov 01, 2024 Protocol ID: MOR-06

The Morrisons system contains a unique sub-system: "Market Street." This provides access to unpackaged, fresh assets (meat, fish, baked goods). This protocol details the optimal method for interfacing with both Market Street and the primary digital loyalty system, "My Morrisons," to exploit the full range of Morrisons offers this week.

Sub-System Alpha: Market Street Interface

Unlike pre-packaged goods, Market Street assets can be acquired in precise quantities. This is the primary advantage. By specifying exact weights or counts, the user can eliminate data surplus (waste). Direct communication with the human operator at the counter is key; they possess real-time data on asset quality and value.

Sub-System Beta: My Morrisons App

The "My Morrisons" application provides personalized, temporary price reductions. It does not operate on a cumulative point system. The protocol for use is binary and linear:

  • Step 1 - Activation: Prior to transaction, the user must manually activate all desired offers within the application's interface. Failure to activate results in a null transaction for that offer.
  • Step 2 - Authentication: At the point-of-sale, the user must present their digital card for scanning. This triggers the application of all activated offers to the final bill.

The Integrated Protocol

The optimal procurement strategy involves a dual approach. Utilize the My Morrisons app for standardized, long-life assets. Simultaneously, interface with the Market Street sub-system for fresh, variable-quantity assets. When assessing quality-to-price ratios on premium assets, a cross-system query against the Waitrose offers this week can provide a valuable market benchmark.
