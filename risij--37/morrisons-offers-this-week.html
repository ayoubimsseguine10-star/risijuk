Report: Value Propositions in Morrisons' Weekly Offers

Data Processed: July 28, 2024 | Category: Retailer Intelligence

Morrisons differentiates itself in the competitive UK grocery market through its "Market Street" concept, an in-store simulation of traditional market stalls. Evaluating the Morrisons offers this week requires assessing both this fresh food proposition and their digital loyalty system.

Proposition 1: "Market Street"

Market Street comprises in-house butcher, baker, and fishmonger counters. This vertically integrated model allows for unique promotions and product offerings not available in the main aisles of pre-packaged goods.

Key Value Areas:

  • Butcher Counter: The primary value driver is often the "3 for Â£12" (or similar) multi-buy on fresh meat and poultry, which facilitates cost-effective bulk purchasing for home freezing.
  • Pizza Counter: A made-to-order pizza service, frequently featuring multi-buy deals that offer a significantly lower price point than takeaway competitors.
  • Salad Bar: A component-based pricing model for creating custom salads, offering a cost-controlled and fresh lunch option.

Proposition 2: My Morrisons Digital Rewards

Morrisons' loyalty program, "My Morrisons," has transitioned from a generic points-based system to one of direct, personalized discounts. Users receive digital coupons in the app, which must be activated prior to shopping. The system rewards consistent purchasing behavior by tailoring offers to individual user data, a similar data-driven model to that used by Sainsbury's Nectar.

Success with this system is contingent on a user's pre-shop routine of checking and activating relevant offers within the app to maximize potential savings.
